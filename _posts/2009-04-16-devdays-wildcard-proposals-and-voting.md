Because it’s not clear to anyone, I want to state that you don’t have to actually be a visitor of the DevDays to be able to vote on the sessions. The only thing you need to do is [register for an account](https://devdays.nl/Registreer.aspx), verify your email address by clicking on the link in the email you receive and you’re allowed to vote. Don’t forget to [vote for my session](https://devdays.nl/Media/Detail.aspx?id=bac6f5d6-64e8-4899-88f5-e27ae5a99425).

Almost all companies use a source control repository to work in a team, but a lot of those don’t use Continuous Integration (CI) to…
* Commit code at least once a day
* Verify everything still works by running unit tests on an integration machine
* Deploy databases and set up test databases for integration tests 
* Create deployment packages for test, acceptance and production environments 
* Be able to verify what the status of the project is 
* Deploy any day, any time 

That’s what I want to explain and show during the developer days. If you want to know more about this, [vote for my session](https://devdays.nl/Media/Detail.aspx?id=bac6f5d6-64e8-4899-88f5-e27ae5a99425).

[Dennis Doomen](http://www.dennisdoomen.net/) will also be able to present you with [a great presentation](https://devdays.nl/Media/Detail.aspx?id=ecf1ca07-6458-47ef-843b-a663ffde8859) on [Test-Driven Development](http://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Test-driven_development) and [SOLID principles](http://butunclebob.com/ArticleS.UncleBob.PrinciplesOfOod) by Robert C. Martin (Uncle Bob) if you vote for him too.

On a site note, it’s funny how I sometimes see the ranking of both sessions drop. Some people seem to vote on a friend or colleague and give a thumb down on my and Dennis Doomen his session. Although it might be tempting to make all our thumb up voters also give a thumb down to all other sessions, it’s also a pretty sad. So [giving me the thumb up](https://devdays.nl/Media/Detail.aspx?id=bac6f5d6-64e8-4899-88f5-e27ae5a99425) will do, thank you. 🙂

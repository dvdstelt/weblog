![](/images/testdriven-net-2-0-rtm/r_reflector_thumb.gif)Some time ago I had a discussion with my colleague [Alex Thissen](http://www.alexthissen.nl/weblog/ "Alex heerst!") about the differences between Visual Studio 2005 (unit) testing, and NUnit and TestDriven.NET. I’m very pro NUnit, but Alex says he can do everything I do with VS2005.I disagreed and we talked about bitchslapping, or perhaps some bitslapping on our weblogs. Nothing came from it though.

Until now…

I just noticed TestDriven.NET 2.0 has been released and it has some awesome features! For n00bs, TestDriven.NET offers Visual Studio integration for NUnit. When you take a look at [Jamie Cansdale](http://weblogs.asp.net/nunitaddin/) his [RTM post](http://weblogs.asp.net/nunitaddin/archive/2006/10/30/TestDriven.NET-2.0.aspx) on his weblog, you’ll notice some awesome features like:
1. Reflector integration  
Reflector is the must-have tool for every developer, and the new integration from TestDriven.NET is just plain **AWESOME**!
2. Code coverage with NCover or Team Coverage
3. Our new best friend, “Repeat Test Run” 🙂
4. Test with .NET 1.1 from within Visual Studio 2005
5. Pluggable unit testing frameworks.
6. TypeMock.NET integration

So what are you waiting for? [Download](http://www.testdriven.net/download.aspx), [download](http://www.testdriven.net/download.aspx), [download](http://www.testdriven.net/download.aspx), [download](http://www.testdriven.net/download.aspx)… [NOW](http://www.testdriven.net/download.aspx)!

Richard Grimes from IDesign has [posted a free workshop](http://www.grimes.demon.co.uk/workshops/fusionWS.htm), kind of a handson lab, that explains how .NET loads it’s assemblies.

The workshop teaches you:

<li>Location of .NET assemblies and how to change the default locations  

<li>Using .NET tools to resolve problems with assembly locations and how to fix broken applications  

<li>Sharing assemblies with the GAC, updating GAC dependencies with publisher policy files, GAC references  

<li>Dynamic loading of assemblies, partial named assemblies</li>

Almost a year ago, January 26, I tried to start a short list of links that I found interesting, and called it [Newsflash](https://bloggingabout.net/2006/01/26/10894). Unfortunately, not much came from it, until now. Here’s #2 😉
**Development** * [CardSpace/InfoCard for .NET 1.1](http://blogs.msdn.com/garretts/archive/2006/11/29/asp-net-1-1-token-decryption.aspx)  
It’s really nice that in the future, dasBlog supports CardSpace under .NET 1.1! Garret Serack, Program Manager on the Federated Identity Team (Cardspace) build this to support CardSpace for dasBlog users who don’t have the luxury of .NET 3.0.I wonder if it’s also working under .NET 2.0, as all/most assemblies should run under 2.0.* [Provider Pattern: A practical guide to decoupling .NET 2.0 applications](http://blogs.clearscreen.com/migs/archive/2006/12/01/4781.aspx)  
My friend Miguel has written a short but very practical introduction into the Provider Pattern that’s widely used throughout ASP.NET. I’ve never seen such short but good explanation on how the Provider Pattern works. I’d suggest he’d do this more often and perhaps workout an example of the pattern.
* [C# 3.0 for mere mortals, part 5: Lambda expressions](http://www.alexthissen.nl/weblog/PermaLink.aspx?guid=a83bedd5-6ce0-41f2-9101-d3cd9a432a29)  
If you haven’t seen Alex Thissen his article series on C# 3.0 for mere mortals, be sure to check them out. He’s a star in explaining things, so if you don’t understand what he’s saying about Lambda expressions, you might think again about being a developer. Just kidding of course. But I do believe after a lot of hobbyists started doing .NET, developing <u>can</u> become an art again with every new version of .NET.
**Miscellaneous** * [Nero 7 Reloaded, Windows Vista Ready](http://www.nero.com/)  
I heard some complaints that Nero didn’t run on Vista. I’m running an older 7.x version and it does work, but now Nero 7 Premium Reloaded is out and it seems to be Vista Ready.
* *Calling Exchange 2007* Recently in The Netherlands there was an introduction of Office 2007, Vista and a very cool demo of Exchange 2007.The presenters are calling Exchange 2007 by phone and it actually reads the messages for them. And it’s just so good at reading them. Be amazed!

I was a bit confused by Dan Fernandez’s post about the [Microsoft Express products being free for a year](http://blogs.msdn.com/danielfe/archive/2005/11/07/489897.aspx). I thought they’d all be free forever. Especially [SQL-Server Express](http://msdn.microsoft.com/vstudio/express/sql/download/) (where’s the 2005 in there?) which would be the replacement for the ever free MSDE.

After first posting a comment with this question, I figured I’d search a little better in the [Express FAQ](http://msdn.microsoft.com/vstudio/express/support/faq/default.aspx). No information about SQL Express being free forever. I thought I’d go look at the pricing questions, if I could see what the product would cost after a year. There it was, the magical paragraph:

> Note that we are also offering SQL Server 2005 Express Edition as a free download, and that this offer is not limited to the same promotional pricing period as Visual Studio Express.

So now you know. 🙂 Look at [this post](http://www.alexthissen.nl/weblog/PermaLink.aspx?guid=054ef92c-8ddb-4121-b9bb-fba374f5f909) by Alex Thissen for more info on SQL-Express.  
 For the other Express products, they actually are free downloads for one year. Good thing is, download ‘m now, be able to use them forever.

I’m currently reading [Behind Closes Doors](http://www.pragmaticprogrammer.com/titles/rdbcd/index.html), written by Johanna Rothman and Esther Derby. It’s released under the Pragmatic Bookshelf label, and they’re a great read. I’ve just finished [Pragmatic Unit Testing](http://www.pragmaticprogrammer.com/starter_kit/utc/index.html) and they had some good info that will come in handy. Recently I downloaded [a video](http://weblogs.asp.net/israelio/archive/2005/12/18/433447.aspx) of a Johanna Rothman presentation on my laptop that I still need to see some day. I decided to search a bit more info she wrote or presented. And have I found some. 
* If you like podcasts, check out the TeamAgile [interviews page](http://www.teamagile.com/mainpages/Interviews.html). She talks in [part 1](http://teamagile.com/interviews/JRothman_AgileIsrael_part_one.zip) and [part 2](http://teamagile.com/interviews/JRothman_AgileIsrael_part_two.zip) about 9 steps each organization might want to consider as they start to move toward the more Agile development process. She discusses daily builds, defining milestones, ranking requirements, etc. 
* On the same page, there’s another talk with Johanna called hiring techies and nerds (a reference to [her book](http://www.amazon.com/gp/product/0932633595/qid=1136969547/sr=8-1/ref=sr_8_xs_ap_i1_xgl14/002-1424660-6215211?n=507846&s=books&v=glance)). It links to [another page](http://www.itconversations.com/shows/detail392.html) where you can also download the [mp3](http://www.itconversations.com/audio/download/ITConversations-392.mp3). 
* Johanna of course has [her own website](http://www.jrothman.com/), including [a weblog](http://www.jrothman.com/weblog/blogger.html), the pragmatic manager [newsletter](http://www.jrothman.com/pragmaticmanager.html) and much more! 

Now if you’re not interested, at least I have the links here for my own reference.

A collegue of mine who’s a graphical designer (or art director, as his card tells us) refered to [an article about Ajax](http://adaptivepath.com/publications/essays/archives/000385.php). According to the article it’s based on HTML, XHTML, CSS, DOM, XML, XSLT, XMLHttpRequest and Javascript binding these technologies together.

I don’t care what the writer has to say about the technologies, but I think he has a point when he says that this is going to be the next big thing in the world of the web development. Or at least I hope so. He refers to [GMail](http://www.gmail.com/), [Google Suggest](http://www.google.com/webhp?complete=1&hl=en) and [Google Maps](http://maps.google.com/). Although non of those websites has an awesome looking design like all Microsoft products have, the user experience certainly rocks my world! I cannot believe how fast GMail and Google Suggest are, how easy it is to work with them.

The article is a good read and it’s certainly something companies can benefit from, when adapted. Especially when they adapt early.
